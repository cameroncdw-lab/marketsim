Fundamentals

Expense Ratios

A fund's annual fee charged as a percentage of your assets. Even small differences compound dramatically over decades — this is one of the most important numbers for long-term investors.

Fidelity 500 Index (FXAIX)0.015%
Vanguard S&P 500 (VOO)0.03%
iShares Core S&P 500 (IVV)0.03%
Typical Actively Managed Fund0.50–0.75%
High-Cost Active / Hedge1.0–2.0%

Example: $10,000 invested for 30 years at 7% return.
At 0.03% ER → $74,872
At 1.50% ER → $57,435
Difference: $17,437 lost to fees

Strategy

Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market price. This reduces the risk of investing a lump sum at the wrong time and removes emotion from the decision.

S&P 500 avg annual return (1924–2024)+10.5%
Worst rolling 10-yr period2000–2009: −0.95%
Best rolling 10-yr period1990–1999: +18.2%
% of rolling 10-yr windows positive~94%
% of rolling 20-yr windows positive100%

Time in the market beats timing the market — staying invested through volatility is more important than finding the "perfect" entry point.

Behavior

Time In Market vs. Timing

The stock market's best days often happen during periods of volatility. Missing just a handful of the best days can cut your returns dramatically.

Fully invested $10k (20 years)~$67,275
Missed best 10 days~$30,896 (−54%)
Missed best 20 days~$17,410 (−74%)
Missed best 30 days~$10,245 (−85%)

7 of the best 10 single days historically occurred within 2 weeks of the 10 worst days. Panic selling costs returns.

Diversification

Asset Correlation

Different asset classes don't always move in the same direction. Mixing them can reduce your overall portfolio volatility while maintaining returns.

Stocks vs BondsLow / Negative
US vs International StocksModerate (0.6–0.8)
Stocks vs GoldVery Low (~0.1)
Large Cap vs Small CapHigh (0.8–0.9)
Bonds vs GoldLow / Slight Positive

A classic 60/40 (stocks/bonds) portfolio has historically delivered ~8.5% returns with significantly lower volatility than an all-stock portfolio.
